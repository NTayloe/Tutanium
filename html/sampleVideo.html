How to Tie a Tie


Step One

Take your tie, and with the seam side down, and place it around your neck. Make sure it rests flat against your body with the seam side down.

Step Two

Here's where we adjust for length. Pull on the fat end so that the skinny end is about at your rib cage. Choosing length is a very important step. We will get to it later.

Step Three

Take the fat end, and cross it over the skinny end, making an X. Take hold of the center of the X, this will becomes the knot. Ensure you have a good grip, we can readjust later.

Step Four

The space between the center of the X and your neck is the "tie hole". Take the tie and make it go out of the hole by placing your hand on the front of the tie and push it straight up through the hole. Pull it all the way through until it is resting in front of you.

Step Five

Now take the tie, and push it behind the hole. The seam should now be facing out. Now grab under the tie, flip it around, and push it through the hole. Once again the seam side should be showing.

Step Six

Take the tie, and pull it over across the hole. Place your finger ontop of the knot while you are pulling the tie across the hole. The face should now be showing.

Step Seven

Take the tie, and push it through the hole again. As you do this, flip it over so the face is showing. Take the fat end, and push it through the knot where your finger was placed in the previous step. Keep pulling the fat end through until the knot is tight. Be sure to not lose the skinny end in this process.

Step Eight

Squeeze the bottom of the knot to ensure the proper knot shape. Grab the skinny end of the tie with one hand, and the bottom of the knot with the other. Pull the skinny end towards the bottom of the tie and the knot towards your neck until it is resting against your collar. Make adjustments based on preference, and you now have now successfully tied a tie.
